My Journey

I entered Iron Hack’s coding boot camp, which gave me experience with all stages of the development cycle for developing dynamic web projects. While enrolled in Iron Hack’s program, I worked as a full-time teacher and infused the knowledge I gained into my classroom’s curriculum by building a fully functional game alongside my students based off of our lesson. I began hosting an afterschool program for young girls who attended my school who gained interest in coding. Soon after the completion of my program at Iron Hack, I began to get involved in local hackathons and meetups around my community to keep myself involved in the tech ecosystem of Miami. I returned to Iron Hack’s school as a course manager, where I currently focus on DRY code principles, debugging, design patterns, and test driven development on applications built with MongoDB, Express, Angular, and Node for 18+ students.
