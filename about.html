How did I get into coding?

One day, at the beginning of my junior year of college, I got what I thought to be the most incredible app idea, atleast for someone like myself. This idea not only drove me crazy from consistent dwelling thoughts, but drove me towards wanting to build it. See, I was a psychology major, which in many ways lends a hand to app design based off human behavior, but also gives me an extensive background on how to handle quantitative and qualitative data when it comes to UX approaches. But... How wold I build this app? I didn't know where or how to start. I'm not even sure I knew much at all about coding. So I started with designing it. By my senior year, I was bold enough to share idea and from peoples astonishment, I pushed forward into finding someone who could develop it for me... got an odd feeling around the idea of someone else building up my idea and eventually decided that I would set out to do it myself. From that moment I never looked back. After graduating from Boston College, I served as a title one teacher where I led the most proud jump in the schools immersive ways to utilize technologies in the classrooms I managed the tech department. My curiosity on building this app grew, so I started at a coding bootcamp and from there not look back. I pushed the curriculum by building my first game with my students. "Bee's vs. Humanity", a game based from our studies on the ecosystem. I finished IronHack awarded with the most Intuitive Design.
